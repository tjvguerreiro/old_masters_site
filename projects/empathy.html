Tiago Guerreiro

Empathy

For every device and application, there are, at a certain time, novice and expert users, less and more able individuals, sufficient and insufficient tools to operate the interface at hand. In our research, we are exploring the concept of “Data Doning”, meaning that one user can choose to install our data doning application and, from thereafter, his/her interaction data (touch data, workflows,…) are shared to a cloud repository and used to improve the interaction attained by others.

One current example is “Assistive Play” where one user can donate his mobile gaming interaction data, tagging actions with names, and disabled users can then use their compounded interactions by associating them with simpler actions. Imagine that one user is playing “Fruit Ninja” where he performs swypes to cut the fruit; a motor impaired person may be able to perform onscreen touches but may not have the precision or reach to perform a swype. The donor records a set of gestures, gives them names and associates those gestures with Fruit Ninja. The disabled person can then chose touch actions (ex: touches on different sides of a tablet can be associated with swipes in different directions). While the overall framework is being built and this scenario can be achieved in theory, motivation to donate data needs to be fostered.

In this project, the student will study how empathy can be created, increased and maintained so that users want to donate data. Approaches include providing awareness of the benefits of the donations made, gamifying data doning, creating in-app awareness for the problems others have.

Evaluations will be performed with older and disabled people along with the mainstream mobile phone user. The framework is to be deployed and used in the wild and made available through Android Play Store.

This student will work in collaboration with researchers from the University of Lisbon (FCUL and IST) and Newcastle University.
