Braille Case

Touchscreen devices have paved their way into the mobile scene, presenting a wide set of possibilities but a comparable number of new challenges, particularly for people who are blind. While they have decreased the number of (fixed) tactile cues, such as buttons, they also increased the freedom to create novel interaction techniques, such as gestures.

The mobile physical braille is a research and development project that aims to combine the benefits of physical keyboards (speed and accuracy) and gestural interfaces (flexibility and personalization). The goal is to develop a 6-button physical keyboard that can be attached to the back of a mainstream smartphone, enabling non-visual chorded-input, while freeing the device’s touchscreen for gestural interaction with the thumbs. The prototype will be built resorting to a fast-prototyping platform, such as Arduino, and will work as an input method on Android devices. We will explore the appropriateness of several gestures while chording in order to active functions such as spellchecking, editing operations, numbers, symbols, etc.

For more information about related projects, visit www.braille21.com.

Outcome: Android input method consisting of a physical keyboard and gestural interaction.

Preferred Skills: Java/Android application development and/or Arduino prototyping.
