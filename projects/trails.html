Tiago Guerreiro

Trails

Mobile devices are nowadays “Swiss Army Knives”. They support a wide range of tasks, enabling people access a wealth of information and services through their extensive connectivity; they have the potential to empower people in everyday tasks. Unfortunately, getting started with a smartphone is not an easy task, particularly for blind users. The tutorials and help systems that come out of the box are not enough for a user to even get started. This masters is aimed to address the problems people face when interacting with a new mobile device by showing users trails from other users interactions.

This project will involve working with Android and, eventually, crowd sourcing platforms. First, you will develop a system that collects user interactions and is able to extract common interaction trails in different applications. Secondly, you will assess if and how different users groups produce different trails (e.g. novice vs expert, blind vs sighted) and explore ways to present interaction trails to different user groups. Finnally, together with the team members you will integrate crowd source tecnhology (e.g. generating and tagging trails). You will work closely with end users (e.g. blind) to assess if and how trails facilitate user interaction and learning process.
